Conclusions

Based on the regression results, we have to conclude that It has not been demonostrated obvious benefits on students' physical fitness from the GFPP yet. However, it does not necessarily mean that GFPP won't have any impacts on students' health. As a matter of fact, nutrition-related policies usually have more significant influences on the health outcomes of younger children and children from poorer families. Unfortunately, due to the limited time we have and the inavailability of individual-level data, we fail to conduct more rigorous quantitative analysis and to refine our project. Thus, given that GFPP is now implemented in Los Angeles, a municipality with relatively better socio-economic environment for merely 4 years, our results are not especiall surprising.

As a result, we hope our project can serve as a reference for researchers to delve more deeply into evaluating the GFPP in the future. With more and more cities adopting GFPP, we believe that there will be even more comprehensive data and resources available in the near future. Hence, we suggest to redo this project with individual-level or household-level data in a few years. We can then expand the evaluation to not only children's health outcomes but also their education attainment.
