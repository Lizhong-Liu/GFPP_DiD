Good Food Purchasing Policy in Los Angeles:
Inferences on Impacts

Regression Results

Choice of dependent variable

Our goal is to measure GFPP's impact on student physical fitness. Among all the 6 stduent fitness areas (table 1) monitored by California state, we are most interested in students' finess change in Body Composition. This is because better food quality and a more balanced diet brought by GFPP do not necessarily tranaslate directly to improved body functions such as adrobic capacity, or improved athletic abilities such as muscle strength. However, we believe changes in students' body composition, such as lower fat percentadge expressed in skinfold measurement or BMI (Body Mass Index), response faster to a diet improvement. Therefore, we expect to see a higher fraction of students meeting the healthy body composition standand a few years after the policy was implemented.

Difference in difference methodology

Our goal is to measure change in physically healthy student fraction caused by GFPP. However, change in body composition, as well as in a school's physically-healthy student fraction, has a lot of attributable factors other than the implementation of GFPP. For example, the overall trend towards healthy eating might have an effect on students' food choice when they eat outside of schools, and in turn leads to a change in students' body composition. Also, a change in school curriculum initiated by educational authorities might influence the sports time of every student in the state of California, and thus have an effect on schools' physically-healthy student fraction. All these factors can mess up our estimation of GFPP on schools' physically-healthy student fraction. However, as these factors are common trend that apply for all schools in LA county, we can estimate their aggregated effect using DiD model.

Since GFPP is only adopted by the Unified School Districts of LA and not in other school districts. We used schools in unified school districts as treatment group and schools in other districts as controlled group. We measure the average physically-healthy students fraction within treatment group and control group. Then we subtract the change in treatment group with the change in control group to get a clean effect of GFPP on physically-healthy student fraction.

Control for covariates

We are aware that socioeconomic attributes of school influence the number of physically-healthy students in the school. Especially, our control and treatment group might not be randomly constructed and that they are not comparable across sosioeconomic attibutes such as schools' size, poverty level, ethnicity diversity, and gender diversity. That being considered, we performed a balance test between treatment group and control group from the following four dimentions.

We are also aware that besides the coraviates we have controlled for, school may have other indiviaully-invariant attributes that we haven't accounted for. For example, schools have different sports facilities, differnet locations that provide access to different environments. These may also influence students physical fitness level.
